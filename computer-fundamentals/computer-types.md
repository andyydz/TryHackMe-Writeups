# Computer Types | TryHackMe Write-up

In this room, I learned that computers are not just laptops and desktops. Computers actually exist almost everywhere around us, including devices we normally do not think of as computers.

Before this room, when I heard the word "computer," I mostly thought about PCs or laptops. But after completing this room, I understood that many everyday devices contain small computer systems designed for specific purposes.

This room also explained why computers come in different types and how their design depends on their intended use.

---

# Computers Exist Everywhere

One of the biggest things I learned is that computers are embedded into many daily-use devices.

Examples include:

- Smartphones
- Smart TVs
- Routers
- Cars
- Coffee machines
- Washing machines
- Smartwatches

Most modern electronic devices contain processors and software that allow them to perform automated tasks.

### Real-world example

A smart TV is basically a specialized computer because it:

- Runs applications
- Connects to the internet
- Processes data
- Uses an operating system

The same applies to smartphones and gaming consoles.

---

# Embedded Systems

This room introduced the concept of embedded systems.

An embedded system is a computer built for a specific purpose instead of general-purpose computing.

Unlike a desktop computer that can perform many tasks, embedded systems are usually designed to handle one main job efficiently.

### Examples

- Microwave ovens
- Traffic lights
- Security cameras
- Printers
- ATMs

### What I understood

These systems usually:

- Use less power
- Have smaller hardware
- Perform limited but optimized tasks

This helped me realize how important small computer systems are in modern technology.

---

# General Purpose Computers

I also learned about general-purpose computers.

These are systems designed to perform multiple different tasks.

Examples:

- Laptops
- Desktop computers
- Smartphones

These devices can:

- Browse the internet
- Run applications
- Play games
- Edit videos
- Write code

Unlike embedded systems, they are more flexible and powerful.

---

# Why Different Computer Types Exist

This section explained why computers are designed differently depending on their purpose.

Not every system needs high performance hardware.

For example:

A smartwatch does not need:

- High-end GPU
- Large cooling system
- Powerful gaming processor

because its tasks are relatively lightweight.

On the other hand:

Gaming PCs and servers require:

- More processing power
- Better cooling
- Larger memory
- Faster storage

### Real-world importance

This made me understand how hardware design depends on:

- Cost
- Power usage
- Performance needs
- Device size
- Intended purpose

---

# Servers and Powerful Systems

The room also touched on larger computer systems like servers.

Servers are powerful computers designed to provide services to other devices over a network.

Examples of server use:

- Hosting websites
- Managing databases
- Cloud storage
- Online gaming servers

### Real-world example

When someone visits YouTube or Instagram, requests are handled by servers running in large data centers.

This helped me understand that modern internet services rely heavily on specialized computer systems working together.

---

# IoT Devices (Internet of Things)

Another interesting concept introduced was IoT devices.

IoT devices are smart devices connected to the internet that can collect or exchange data.

Examples:

- Smart bulbs
- Smart speakers
- Smart door locks
- Fitness trackers

### What I understood

Even small devices today can:

- Connect to networks
- Process data
- Communicate with other systems

From a cybersecurity perspective, this is important because insecure IoT devices can become attack targets.

---

# How This Relates To Cybersecurity

This room also made me realize that cybersecurity is not only about protecting laptops or servers.

Many different types of devices can become vulnerable targets, including:

- Smart home devices
- Cameras
- Routers
- Embedded systems

Understanding different computer types helps in understanding:

- Attack surfaces
- Hardware limitations
- Device security risks
